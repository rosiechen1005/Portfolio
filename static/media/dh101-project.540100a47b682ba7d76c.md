# Social Networks & College Students' Mental Health

**A data-driven digital humanities project** exploring how social networks relate to mental health outcomes among college students—from data sourcing and critique through processing and interactive presentation.

---

## What this project demonstrates

This project showcases **end-to-end data literacy** and **presentation skills** relevant to data science and analytics roles:

| Area | What we do |
|------|------------|
| **Data critique & quality** | Provenance, schema documentation, missingness, bias, and fitness-for-purpose evaluation (Data Critique page). |
| **Processing & pipeline thinking** | Documented methodology for cleaning, feature engineering, network construction (nodes/edges), and ethical handling of sensitive data (About · Methodology). |
| **Visualization & dashboards** | Interactive diagrams, timelines, and dashboards to communicate findings clearly and accessibly. |
| **Ethics & reproducibility** | Explicit attention to consent, anonymization, limitations, and replicable acquisition/cleaning steps. |
| **Documentation & communication** | Structured narrative (sources → processing → presentation), citations, and clear documentation of choices and tradeoffs. |

**Tech:** Jekyll, GitHub Pages, HTML/CSS/JS—focused on clear, accessible presentation and extensible visualizations.
